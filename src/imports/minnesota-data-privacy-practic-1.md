Organizations operating in Minnesota must develop privacy policies that comply with these laws while addressing breach notification requirements, sector-specific rules, and evolving enforcement standards. This document provides best practices for drafting and implementing compliant data privacy policies under Minnesota law.\n\n---\n\n## I. Overview of Minnesota's Data Privacy Framework\n\n### A. Minnesota Consumer Data Privacy Act (MCDPA)\n\nThe MCDPA applies to legal entities that conduct business in Minnesota or produce products or services targeted to Minnesota residents and satisfy one of the following thresholds:\n\n1. **Processing volume threshold**: Control or process personal data of 100,000 or more consumers during a calendar year (excluding data processed solely for payment transactions); or\n\n2. **Revenue threshold**: Derive over 25% of gross revenue from the sale of personal data and process or control personal data of 25,000 or more consumers. ([20C Minn. Prac., Bus. Reg. Stat. § 13.02\n- Federally recognized Indian tribes\n- Protected health information under HIPAA\n- Information regulated under the Fair Credit Reporting Act\n- Data covered by the Minnesota Insurance Fair Information Reporting Act\n- Payment-only transactions where no consumer data is retained ([Minn. Stat. Ann. § 325M.12 (West)](https://1.next.westlaw.com/Document/NC98CFA00228C11EF9CA29401BEC0E94F/View/FullText.html \"@stat.msa3253.1\"); [Minn. Stat. Ann. § 325M.12 (West)](https://1.next.westlaw.com/Document/NC98CFA00228C11EF9CA29401BEC0E94F/View/FullText.html \"@stat.msa3253.2\"); [Minn. Stat. Ann. § 325M.12 (West)](https://1.next.westlaw.com/Document/NC98CFA00228C11EF9CA29401BEC0E94F/View/FullText.html \"@stat.msa3253.3\"))\n\n### B. Minnesota Government Data Practices Act (MGDPA)\n\nThe MGDPA regulates government entities' collection, creation, storage, maintenance, dissemination, and access to government data. ([Minn. Stat. Ann. § 13.01 (West)](https://1.next.westlaw.com/Document/N5D9F54E0951011DB9BCF9DAC28345A2A/View/FullText.html \"@stat.msa13.2\")) The Act establishes a presumption that government data are public unless classified otherwise by federal law, state statute, or temporary classification. ([20A1 Minn. Prac., Business Law Deskbook § 14:31](https://1.next.westlaw.com/Document/I1d8c49ee98c011ee917c80751dac9729/View/FullText.html \"@sec.a.2\"))\n\nThe MGDPA's purpose is \"to reconcile the rights of data subjects to protect personal information from indiscriminate disclosure with the right of the public to know what the government is doing.\" ([23 Minn. Prac., Trial Handbook For Minn. Lawyers § 23:23 (2025-2026 ed.)](https://1.next.westlaw.com/Document/I3234f55ab74911ddad9288e6e742e17a/View/FullText.html \"@sec.minn.1\"))\n\n---\n\n## II. Privacy Notice Requirements\n\n### A. Required Disclosures Under the MCDPA\n\nControllers must provide consumers with a **reasonably accessible, clear, and meaningful privacy notice** that includes:\n\n1. **Categories of personal data** processed by the controller\n2. **Purposes** for which categories of personal data are processed\n3. **Consumer rights explanation** including how and where consumers may exercise those rights and appeal controller actions\n4. **Categories of personal data sold or shared** with third parties\n5. **Categories of third parties** receiving personal data\n6. **Contact information** including an active email address or other online mechanism\n7. **Retention policies** describing how long personal data is kept\n8. **Effective date** of the privacy notice ([20C Minn. Prac., Bus. Reg. In Minnesota--State 325M.16 (2025-2026 ed.)](https://1.next.westlaw.com/Document/I049e1159e00d11f0ad3895d29d007c02/View/FullText.html \"@sec.c5.1\"))\n\n### B. Special Disclosure Requirements\n\nIf a controller **sells personal data**, **processes data for targeted advertising**, or **engages in profiling** that produces legal or similarly significant effects, the controller must:\n\n- Disclose the processing in the privacy notice\n- Provide a clear and conspicuous opt-out method outside the privacy notice\n- Consider using an Internet hyperlink labeled \"Your Opt-Out Rights\" or \"Your Privacy Rights\" ([Minn. Stat. Ann. § 325M.16 (West)](https://1.next.westlaw.com/Document/ND85C05D0228C11EF8A1C827E8BA37429/View/FullText.html \"@stat.msa3257.2\"))\n\n### C. Accessibility and Language Requirements\n\nThe privacy notice must be:\n- **Multilingual**: Available in each language in which the controller provides products or services\n- **Accessible**: Reasonably accessible to and usable by individuals with disabilities\n- **Updated**: Material changes must be communicated to affected consumers with reasonable opportunity to withdraw consent for prospective data collection ([20C Minn. Prac., Bus. Reg. In Minnesota--State 325M.16 (2025-2026 ed.)](https://1.next.westlaw.com/Document/I049e1159e00d11f0ad3895d29d007c02/View/FullText.html \"@sec.c5.1\"))\n\n### D. Best Practice: Single Harmonized Notice\n\nA controller is not required to provide a separate Minnesota-specific privacy notice if the controller's general privacy notice contains all required information. ([Minn. Stat. Ann. § 325M.16 (West)](https://1.next.westlaw.com/Document/ND85C05D0228C11EF8A1C827E8BA37429/View/FullText.html \"@stat.msa3257.3\")) However, organizations should review their notices against all applicable state privacy laws to ensure comprehensive coverage. ([Practical Law: Reviewing Privacy Notices for US State Consumer Privacy Law Compliance Checklist](https://1.next.westlaw.com/Document/I776e96134f0111ed8636e1a02dc72ff6/View/FullText.html \"@pl.reviewing.1\"))\n\n---\n\n## III. Consumer Rights\n\n### A. Core Consumer Rights Under the MCDPA\n\nMinnesota consumers have the following rights regarding their personal data:\n\n| Right | Description |\n|-------|-------------|\n| **Right to Correct** | Correct inaccurate personal data concerning the consumer ([Minn. Stat. Ann. § 325M.14 (West)](https://1.next.westlaw.com/Document/ND1C88540228C11EF82B9DCBAA0F795EE/View/FullText.html \"@stat.msa3255.1\")) |\n| **Right to Delete** | Delete personal data concerning the consumer ([Minn. Stat. Ann. § 325M.14 (West)](https://1.next.westlaw.com/Document/ND1C88540228C11EF82B9DCBAA0F795EE/View/FullText.html \"@stat.msa3255.3\")) |\n| **Right to Access** | Obtain access to personal data held by the controller |\n| **Right to Opt Out** | Opt out of targeted advertising and sale of personal data ([Minn. Stat. Ann. § 325M.14 (West)](https://1.next.westlaw.com/Document/ND1C88540228C11EF82B9DCBAA0F795EE/View/FullText.html \"@stat.msa3255.2\")) |\n| **Right to Data Portability** | Obtain personal data in a portable format |\n\n### B. Universal Opt-Out Mechanisms\n\nControllers must allow consumers to opt out through an **opt-out preference signal** that:\n- Does not unfairly disadvantage other controllers\n- Requires affirmative, freely given, and unambiguous choice (no default settings)\n- Is consumer-friendly and easy to use\n- Is consistent with similar mechanisms required by other laws\n- Enables accurate determination of Minnesota residency (IP address estimation is sufficient) ([20C Minn. Prac., Bus. Reg. In Minnesota--State 325M.14 (2025-2026 ed.)](https://1.next.westlaw.com/Document/I049e1153e00d11f0ad3895d29d007c02/View/FullText.html \"@sec.c6.1\"))\n\n### C. Responding to Consumer Requests\n\nControllers must establish submission methods that include, at minimum:\n- An actively monitored email address\n- A webform or online portal (if operating a website)\n- A toll-free phone number (unless online-only with direct consumer relationships) ([Practical Law: Reviewing Privacy Notices for US State Consumer Privacy Law Compliance Checklist](https://1.next.westlaw.com/Document/I776e96134f0111ed8636e1a02dc72ff6/View/FullText.html \"@pl.reviewing.3\"))\n\n---\n\n## IV. Data Collection and Use Limitations\n\n### A. Data Minimization Principle\n\nControllers must **limit collection** of personal data to what is:\n- Adequate\n- Relevant\n- Reasonably necessary in relation to the disclosed purposes ([20C Minn. Prac., Bus. Reg. In Minnesota--State 325M.16 (2025-2026 ed.)](https://1.next.westlaw.com/Document/I049e1159e00d11f0ad3895d29d007c02/View/FullText.html \"@sec.c5.2\"))\n\n### B. Purpose Limitation\n\nControllers may not process personal data for purposes that are not reasonably necessary to, or compatible with, the disclosed purposes unless the controller obtains consumer consent. ([20C Minn. Prac., Bus. Reg. In Minnesota--State 325M.16 (2025-2026 ed.)](https://1.next.westlaw.com/Document/I049e1159e00d11f0ad3895d29d007c02/View/FullText.html \"@sec.c5.2\"))\n\n### C. Retention Limitations\n\nControllers may not retain personal data that is no longer relevant and reasonably necessary for the purposes for which it was collected, unless retention is required by law or permitted under statutory exceptions. ([20C Minn. Prac., Bus. Reg. In Minnesota--State 325M.18 (2025-2026 ed.)](https://1.next.westlaw.com/Document/I049e115fe00d11f0ad3895d29d007c02/View/FullText.html \"@sec.c.4\"))\n\n### D. Processing Sensitive Data\n\n**Sensitive data** requires heightened protections:\n\n- Controllers may not process sensitive data without **obtaining consumer consent**\n- For data concerning children, consent must come from the **parent or lawful guardian** in accordance with COPPA requirements\n- Controllers processing data of consumers aged 13–16 may not engage in targeted advertising or sell personal data without consent ([20C Minn. Prac., Bus. Reg. In Minnesota--State 325M.16 (2025-2026 ed.)](https://1.next.westlaw.com/Document/I049e1159e00d11f0ad3895d29d007c02/View/FullText.html \"@sec.c5.2\"))\n\n### E. Nondiscrimination Requirements\n\nControllers shall not process personal data in a manner that unlawfully discriminates based on:\n- Race, color, or ethnicity\n- Religion or national origin\n- Sex, gender, or gender identity\n- Sexual orientation\n- Familial status\n- Lawful source of income\n- Disability\n\nThis prohibition applies with respect to housing, employment, credit, education, and public accommodations. ([Minn. Stat. Ann. § 325M.16 (West)](https://1.next.westlaw.com/Document/ND85C05D0228C11EF8A1C827E8BA37429/View/FullText.html \"@stat.msa3257.1\"))\n\n---\n\n## V. Data Security Requirements\n\n### A. Security Standards\n\nControllers must establish, implement, and maintain **reasonable administrative, technical, and physical data security practices** to protect:\n- **Confidentiality** of personal data\n- **Integrity** of personal data\n- **Accessibility** of personal data\n\nThis includes maintaining an inventory of data that must be managed. Security practices must be appropriate to the volume and nature of the personal data. ([20C Minn. Prac., Bus. Reg. In Minnesota--State 325M.16 (2025-2026 ed.)](https://1.next.westlaw.com/Document/I049e1159e00d11f0ad3895d29d007c02/View/FullText.html \"@sec.c5.2\"))\n\n### B. MGDPA Security Requirements for Government Entities\n\nGovernment entities must:\n1. **Establish procedures** to ensure data on individuals is accurate, complete, and current\n2. **Establish appropriate security safeguards** for all records containing data on individuals\n3. **Develop a policy** incorporating these procedures, which may include model policies for data sharing ([20C Minn. Prac., Bus. Reg. In Minnesota--State 13.05 (2024 ed.)](https://1.next.westlaw.com/Document/I04bf49feca1211ec9867adfd37249c31/View/FullText.html \"@sec.c7.2\"))\n\nWhen disposing of non-public data, the data must be **destroyed in a way that prevents its contents from being determined**. ([20C Minn. Prac., Bus. Reg. In Minnesota--State 13.05 (2024 ed.)](https://1.next.westlaw.com/Document/I04bf49feca1211ec9867adfd37249c31/View/FullText.html \"@sec.c7.2\"))\n\n### C. Data Practices Compliance Official\n\nGovernment entities must designate a **data practices compliance official**—an employee to whom persons may direct questions or concerns regarding data access or other data practices problems. ([20C Minn. Prac., Bus. Reg. In Minnesota--State 13.05 (2024 ed.)](https://1.next.westlaw.com/Document/I04bf49feca1211ec9867adfd37249c31/View/FullText.html \"@sec.c7.3\"))\n\n---\n\n## VI. Data Breach Notification\n\n### A. Private Sector Requirements (Minn. Stat. § 325E.61)\n\nAny person or business conducting business in Minnesota that owns or licenses data including personal information must disclose any breach of security to Minnesota residents whose unencrypted personal information was, or is reasonably believed to have been, acquired by an unauthorized person. ([Minn. Stat. Ann. § 325E.61 (West)](https://1.next.westlaw.com/Document/N098D5270A7FE11DBB5DDAC3692B918BC/View/FullText.html \"@stat.msa3259.1\"))\n\n**Timing**: Disclosure must be made in the most expedient time possible and without unreasonable delay, consistent with:\n- Legitimate needs of law enforcement\n- Measures necessary to determine the scope of the breach\n- Identification of affected individuals\n- Restoration of reasonable data system integrity ([Minn. Stat. Ann. § 325E.61 (West)](https://1.next.westlaw.com/Document/N098D5270A7FE11DBB5DDAC3692B918BC/View/FullText.html \"@stat.msa3259.1\"))\n\n**Service provider notification**: Entities that maintain data they do not own must notify the data owner or licensee of any breach **immediately following discovery**. ([Minn. Stat. Ann. § 325E.61 (West)](https://1.next.westlaw.com/Document/N098D5270A7FE11DBB5DDAC3692B918BC/View/FullText.html \"@stat.msa3259.2\"))\n\n### B. Definition of Breach\n\n\"Breach of the security of the system\" means unauthorized acquisition of computerized data that compromises the security, confidentiality, or integrity of personal information. **Good faith acquisition** by an employee or agent for business purposes is not a breach, provided the information is not used or subject to further unauthorized disclosure. ([Minn. Stat. Ann. § 325E.61 (West)](https://1.next.westlaw.com/Document/N098D5270A7FE11DBB5DDAC3692B918BC/View/FullText.html \"@stat.msa3259.3\"))\n\n### C. Government Entity Requirements (Minn. Stat. § 13.055)\n\nGovernment entities must:\n1. **Disclose breaches** to individuals whose private or confidential data was compromised\n2. **Provide written notification** informing individuals that a report will be prepared\n3. **Prepare an investigation report** including:\n   - Description of the type of data accessed\n   - Number of individuals affected ([Minn. Stat. Ann. § 13.055 (West)](https://1.next.westlaw.com/Document/N55485130192311E495FFF92A3028DD96/View/FullText.html \"@stat.msa1313.1\"); [Minn. Stat. Ann. § 13.055 (West)](https://1.next.westlaw.com/Document/N55485130192311E495FFF92A3028DD96/View/FullText.html \"@stat.msa1313.2\"); [Minn. Stat. Ann. § 13.055 (West)](https://1.next.westlaw.com/Document/N55485130192311E495FFF92A3028DD96/View/FullText.html \"@stat.msa1313.3\"))\n\n### D. Payment Card Data Requirements (Minn. Stat. § 325E.64)\n\nEntities accepting access devices in transactions are **prohibited from retaining**:\n- Card security code data\n- PIN verification code numbers\n- Full contents of magnetic stripe data\n\nRetention is prohibited subsequent to transaction authorization (or 48 hours for PIN debit transactions). ([Minn. Stat. Ann. § 325E.64 (West)](https://1.next.westlaw.com/Document/N58514300766411DCA61EE3CBF801C46A/View/FullText.html \"@stat.msa325.1\"))\n\nViolators are liable for costs incurred by financial institutions, including notification of affected cardholders. ([Minn. Stat. Ann. § 325E.64 (West)](https://1.next.westlaw.com/Document/N58514300766411DCA61EE3CBF801C46A/View/FullText.html \"@stat.msa325.2\"))\n\n---\n\n## VII. Data Privacy and Protection Assessments\n\n### A. When Assessments Are Required\n\nControllers must conduct and document a **data privacy and protection assessment** for each of the following processing activities:\n\n1. Processing personal data for **targeted advertising**\n2. **Sale of personal data**\n3. Processing **sensitive data**\n4. Processing activities presenting a **heightened risk of harm** to consumers\n5. **Profiling** that presents reasonably foreseeable risk of:\n   - Unfair or deceptive treatment or disparate impact\n   - Financial, physical, or reputational injury\n   - Intrusion upon solitude, seclusion, or private affairs\n   - Other substantial injury to consumers ([20C Minn. Prac., Bus. Reg. In Minnesota--State 325M.18 (2025-2026 ed.)](https://1.next.westlaw.com/Document/I049e115fe00d11f0ad3895d29d007c02/View/FullText.html \"@sec.c.3\"))\n\n### B. Assessment Requirements\n\nEach assessment must:\n- **Consider the type of personal data** to be processed, including whether it is sensitive data\n- **Consider the context** in which the personal data is processed\n- **Identify and weigh benefits and risks**—benefits flowing to the controller, consumer, and public against potential risks to consumer rights\n- **Factor in** use of deidentified data, reasonable consumer expectations, and the controller-consumer relationship ([20C Minn. Prac., Bus. Reg. In Minnesota--State 325M.18 (2025-2026 ed.)](https://1.next.westlaw.com/Document/I049e115fe00d11f0ad3895d29d007c02/View/FullText.html \"@sec.c.2\"); [20C Minn. Prac., Bus. Reg. In Minnesota--State 325M.18 (2025-2026 ed.)](https://1.next.westlaw.com/Document/I049e115fe00d11f0ad3895d29d007c02/View/FullText.html \"@sec.c.6\"))\n\n### C. Documentation and Compliance\n\nControllers must document and maintain:\n1. **Chief Privacy Officer or designee** contact information\n2. **Description of data privacy policies and procedures** including:\n   - Requirements reflected in system design\n   - Procedures for identifying and providing personal data to consumers\n   - Administrative, technical, and physical security practices\n   - Data minimization procedures\n   - Retention policies\n   - Violation identification and remediation procedures ([20C Minn. Prac., Bus. Reg. In Minnesota--State 325M.18 (2025-2026 ed.)](https://1.next.westlaw.com/Document/I049e115fe00d11f0ad3895d29d007c02/View/FullText.html \"@sec.c.4\"))\n\nAssessments conducted for compliance with other laws may qualify if they have **similar scope and effect**. ([20C Minn. Prac., Bus. Reg. In Minnesota--State 325M.18 (2025-2026 ed.)](https://1.next.westlaw.com/Document/I049e115fe00d11f0ad3895d29d007c02/View/FullText.html \"@sec.c.5\"))\n\n---\n\n## VIII. Processor and Third-Party Obligations\n\n### A. Processor Contracts\n\nControllers must enter into contracts with processors governing data processing procedures. Key provisions should address:\n- Instructions for processing personal data\n- Nature and purpose of processing\n- Type of data subject to processing\n- Duration of processing\n- Rights and obligations of both parties\n\n### B. Protections for Deidentified and Pseudonymous Data\n\n- Controllers using pseudonymous or deidentified data must exercise **reasonable oversight** to monitor compliance with contractual commitments\n- Controllers must take appropriate steps to address breaches of contractual commitments ([20C Minn. Prac., Bus. Reg. In Minnesota--State 325M.15 (2025-2026 ed.)](https://1.next.westlaw.com/Document/I049e1156e00d11f0ad3895d29d007c02/View/FullText.html \"@sec.c4.2\"))\n- Processors and third parties must **not attempt to reidentify** deidentified or pseudonymous data without express controller authority ([20C Minn. Prac., Bus. Reg. In Minnesota--State 325M.15 (2025-2026 ed.)](https://1.next.westlaw.com/Document/I049e1156e00d11f0ad3895d29d007c02/View/FullText.html \"@sec.c4.3\"); [20C Minn. Prac., Bus. Reg. In Minnesota--State 325M.15 (2025-2026 ed.)](https://1.next.westlaw.com/Document/I049e1156e00d11f0ad3895d29d007c02/View/FullText.html \"@sec.c4.4\"))\n\n### C. Third-Party Liability Shield\n\nA controller that discloses personal data to a third party in compliance with the MCDPA is **not liable** for the recipient's violations, provided the controller did not have actual knowledge of the recipient's intent to violate the law. ([20C Minn. Prac., Bus. Reg. In Minnesota--State 325M.19 (2025-2026 ed.)](https://1.next.westlaw.com/Document/I049e1162e00d11f0ad3895d29d007c02/View/FullText.html \"@sec.c2.3\"))\n\n---\n\n## IX. Enforcement and Penalties\n\n### A. Attorney General Authority\n\nThe Minnesota Attorney General has authority to investigate and enforce the MCDPA. As part of civil investigative demands, the Attorney General may request disclosure of **data privacy and protection assessments** relevant to the investigation. ([20C Minn. Prac., Bus. Reg. In Minnesota--State 325M.18 (2025-2026 ed.)](https://1.next.westlaw.com/Document/I049e115fe00d11f0ad3895d29d007c02/View/FullText.html \"@sec.c.1\"))\n\nAssessments disclosed to the Attorney General:\n- Are classified as **nonpublic data** under Minn. Stat. § 13.02\n- **Do not waive** attorney-client privilege or work product protection ([20C Minn. Prac., Bus. Reg. In Minnesota--State 325M.18 (2025-2026 ed.)](https://1.next.westlaw.com/Document/I049e115fe00d11f0ad3895d29d007c02/View/FullText.html \"@sec.c.1\"))\n\n### B. MGDPA Penalties\n\nIn determining whether to assess civil penalties for MGDPA violations, courts consider whether the government entity has substantially complied with the Act, including whether it has:\n- Designated a data practices compliance official\n- Prepared a data inventory\n- Developed public access procedures\n- Established procedures to guarantee data subject rights\n- Implemented procedures to ensure data accuracy and security ([Minn. Stat. Ann. § 13.08 (West)](https://1.next.westlaw.com/Document/NF07D6BB039A411E69D65EC9FC0DD0DC9/View/FullText.html \"@stat.msa132.1\"))\n\n---\n\n## X. Sector-Specific Considerations\n\n### A. Health Data\n\nOrganizations handling health information must comply with:\n- **HIPAA** requirements for protected health information\n- **Minn. Stat. § 144.291** defining health records\n- Mental health data protections under **Minn. Stat. § 13.46**, which classifies mental health data as private and restricts disclosure ([Minn. Stat. Financial Data\n\nFinancial institutions must consider:\n- **Gramm-Leach-Bliley Act (GLBA)** requirements\n- Minnesota payment card data retention prohibitions under **Minn. Stat. § 325E.64** ([Minn. Genetic Data\n\nDirect-to-consumer genetic testing companies must safeguard genetic data by providing consumers processes to:\n- Access their genetic data\n- Delete their account and genetic data ([Minn. Stat. Ann. § 325F.995 (West)](https://1.next.westlaw.com/Document/ND97FD43016E111EE918CD75B3C528D76/View/FullText.html \"@stat.msa3254.1\"); [Minn. Stat. Educational Data\n\nTechnology providers maintaining educational data must:\n- Comply with **Minn. Ann. § 13.32 (West)](https://1.next.westlaw.com/Document/N9243EB71582E11F08C2FB6976281D584/View/FullText.html \"@stat.msa1314.1\"))\n\n### E. Employment Data\n\nPersonnel data is generally private under the MGDPA, with limited exceptions for public employees. ([20A1 Minn. Ann. § 13.43 (West)](https://1.next.westlaw.com/Document/NE0374B10421211F08BA6ABE8987EF78A/View/FullText.html \"@stat.msa137.1\"))\n- Personal contact information of contractor or subcontractor employees ([Minn. Practical Recommendations for Privacy Policy Drafting\n\n### A. Core Privacy Program Components\n\nAn effective Minnesota privacy compliance program should include:\n\n1. **Data Mapping**: Understand the types of personal data collected, where it is stored, how it flows through the organization, and who has access ([Practical Law: Developing a Privacy Compliance Program](https://1.next.westlaw.com/Document/I8050df6c516811e598dc8b09b4f043e0/View/FullText.html \"@pl.developing.2\"))\n\n2. **Legal Requirements Analysis**: Determine which Minnesota laws apply based on:\n   - Type of information collected\n   - Jurisdictions of operation\n   - Nature of data use or treatment ([Practical Law: Developing a Privacy Compliance Program](https://1.next.westlaw.com/Document/I8050df6c516811e598dc8b09b4f043e0/View/FullText.html \"@pl.developing.3\"))\n\n3. **Privacy Notice Development**: Create notices that are:\n   - Clear and concise\n   - Multilingual where required\n   - Accessible to individuals with disabilities\n   - Accurate and updated as practices change\n\n4. **Internal Policies and Procedures**: Document compliance procedures including:\n   - Data collection and retention standards\n   - Security safeguards\n   - Consumer rights request handling\n   - Breach response protocols\n\n### B. Privacy Notice Checklist\n\nBefore publishing a privacy notice, ensure it addresses:\n\n| Element | Requirement |\n|---------|-------------|\n| Data categories | List all categories of personal data collected |\n| Processing purposes | Explain why each category is collected |\n| Third-party sharing | Identify categories of recipients |\n| Consumer rights | Describe rights and how to exercise them |\n| Opt-out mechanisms | Provide clear methods for opting out of sales and targeted advertising |\n| Contact information | Include active email and other contact methods |\n| Retention periods | Describe data retention policies |\n| Security measures | Summarize protective measures |\n| Effective date | Include date of last update |\n\n### C. Consent Best Practices\n\nWhen consent is required:\n- Ensure consent is **affirmative, freely given, and unambiguous**\n- **Avoid pre-checked boxes** or default settings\n- Provide an **equally easy mechanism to revoke consent**\n- Cease processing within **15 days** of revocation request ([20C Minn. Prac., Bus. Reg. In Minnesota--State 325M.16 (2025-2026 ed.)](https://1.next.westlaw.com/Document/I049e1159e00d11f0ad3895d29d007c02/View/FullText.html \"@sec.c5.2\"))\n\n### D. Government Entity Compliance\n\nGovernment entities should:\n1. Designate a **data practices compliance official** by name and contact information\n2. Prepare and maintain a **data inventory**\n3. Develop **public access procedures**\n4. Implement **Tennessen warning** procedures when collecting private or confidential data\n5. Establish security safeguards appropriate to data classification ([20C Minn. Prac., Bus. Reg. In Minnesota--State 13.05 (2024 ed.)](https://1.next.westlaw.com/Document/I04bf49feca1211ec9867adfd37249c31/View/FullText.html \"@sec.c7.2\"); [20C Minn. Prac., Bus. Reg. In Minnesota--State 13.05 (2024 ed.)](https://1.next.westlaw.com/Document/I04bf49feca1211ec9867adfd37249c31/View/FullText.html \"@sec.c7.3\"))\n\nThe **Tennessen warning** must inform individuals of:\n- The purpose and intended use of requested data\n- Whether the individual may refuse or is legally required to supply data\n- Consequences of supplying or refusing to supply data\n- Identity of other entities authorized to receive the data ([Minn. Stat. Ann. § 13.04 (West)](https://1.next.westlaw.com/Document/NF1C7758046AE11F09A379B60770500A7/View/FullText.html \"@stat.msa138.4\"))\n\n---\n\n## XII. Key Compliance Dates and Thresholds\n\n| Requirement | Threshold/Date |\n|------------|----------------|\n| MCDPA applicability | 100,000+ consumers processed OR 25% revenue from data sales + 25,000+ consumers |\n| Response to consumer requests | Timeframes specified in the statute |\n| Consent revocation processing | Within 15 days of receipt |\n| Privacy notice updates | Upon any material change to practices |\n| Data disposal | Destroy non-public data to prevent content determination |\n\n---\n\n## XIII. Exemptions and Limitations\n\n### A. Processing Exemptions\n\nControllers and processors are not restricted from:\n- Complying with federal, state, or local laws\n- Responding to civil, criminal, or regulatory inquiries\n- Cooperating with law enforcement\n- Investigating or defending legal claims\n- Performing contracts with consumers\n- Protecting life or physical safety\n- Preventing fraud, security incidents, or illegal activity\n- Assisting other controllers or processors with these obligations\n- Engaging in approved public interest research\n- Processing data for public health purposes ([20C Minn. Prac., Bus. Reg. In Minnesota--State 325M.19 (2025-2026 ed.)](https://1.next.westlaw.com/Document/I049e1162e00d11f0ad3895d29d007c02/View/FullText.html \"@sec.c2.2\"))\n\n### B. Evidentiary Privilege Protection\n\nMCDPA obligations do not apply where compliance would violate an evidentiary privilege under Minnesota law. ([20C Minn. Prac., Bus. Reg. In Minnesota--State 325M.19 (2025-2026 ed.)](https://1.next.westlaw.com/Document/I049e1162e00d11f0ad3895d29d007c02/View/FullText.html \"@sec.c2.1\"))\n\n### C. Data Subject Burden\n\nIf a controller processes personal data pursuant to an exemption, the controller bears the burden of demonstrating that the processing qualifies for the exemption. ([20C Minn. Prac., Bus. Reg. In Minnesota--State 325M.19 (2025-2026 ed.)](https://1.next.westlaw.com/Document/I049e1162e00d11f0ad3895d29d007c02/View/FullText.html \"@sec.c2.6\"))\n\n---\n\n## XIV. Conclusion\n\nMinnesota's data privacy framework imposes substantial obligations on both private entities and government agencies. Organizations should:\n\n1. **Assess applicability** by determining whether they meet MCDPA thresholds or are subject to the MGDPA\n2. **Develop comprehensive privacy notices** meeting all statutory disclosure requirements\n3. **Implement robust consumer rights processes** including universal opt-out mechanisms\n4. **Conduct required privacy assessments** for high-risk processing activities\n5. **Establish security safeguards** appropriate to the nature and volume of data\n6. **Prepare breach response plans** to meet expeditious notification requirements\n7. **Document compliance** through written policies, procedures, and assessments\n\nRegular review and updating of privacy policies and practices is essential as Minnesota's data privacy landscape continues to evolve through regulatory guidance and enforcement actions.\n\n---\n\n*This document is intended to provide general guidance on Minnesota data privacy law compliance.
